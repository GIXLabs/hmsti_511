# Overview
For the rest of the quarter, you will be applying all that you've learned so far to develop a physical product that solves a problem that you have. It should idealy be extremely niche, to the point where you can't just buy a similar solution off of Amazon.

This assignment is to propose a few different solutions that you will develop over the remainder of the quarter. Pick 1 problem to solve and sketch at least 3 dramatically different solutions to that problem. 

# Learning Objectives
By the end of this assignment you will be able to:
- Identify problems that can be solved with physical objects.
- Brainstorm multiple ideas to solve a given problem.
- Select the most feasible option for solving a problem to try first.
- Identify where uncertainty exists in your solution so you can test that first.

# Constraints
- All sketches and 3D models must be made by you.
- Your 3 solutions should be dramatically different. (See FAQs for more details)
- You must describe your ideas well enough that someone else can understand them.
    - Ask a friend to tell you what they think you mean.

# Deliverables (Quiz format)
1. Please describe the problem you are going to attempt to solve.
1. Import your sketches for your first solution and add any text you think would be helpful in understanding your solution. What is the biggest source of uncertainty in this solution?
1. Import your sketches for your second solution and add any text you think would be helpful in understanding your solution. What is the biggest source of uncertainty in this solution?
1. Import your sketches for your third solution and add any text you think would be helpful in understanding your solution. What is the biggest source of uncertainty in this solution?

# FAQs
- What does "dramatically different solutions" really mean?
    - If you were planning a trip to Portland, dramatically different solutions would be:
        - Driving
        - Renting a boat and sailing
        - Taking a train
    - The following is the opposite of what I'm looking for:
        - Flying with Alaska Airlines
        - Flying with Delta Airlines
        - Flying with Frontier Airlines
    - When in doubt, ask a member of the instructional team whether your ideas are different enough.
