# Overview
Could you make and sell your light idea profitably? Get a quote from a 3D printing provider of your choice for 50 of your light idea (see [Manufacturing Quotes Assignment](manu_quotes.md)). Include the estimated shipping cost as part of the total cost. Also include an estimate of how long it would take to assemble and package, then multiply that by $25/hr. Then calculate what you would need to sell your item for to have 50% margins (Note: don't just multiply by 1.5, this will be wrong).

Write up your reasoning on whether you could sell your product at this price.

# Learning Objectives
By the end of this activity you will be able to do the following:
- Evaluate the cost to produce one of your designs.
- Make realistic judgments about the feasibility of selling your product profitably.

# Constraints
- Your calculations must include all of the parts designed by you.
    - If you have a combination of lasercut and 3D printed parts, you may need to get quotes from separate vendors and add them together.
    - Don't include the cost of fasteners (like screws).

# Deliverables
In a single pdf:
- Screenshot of your quote for enough parts to make 50 of your design.
- Calculation of per unit cost without labor.
- Calculation of per unit cost with labor.
- Calculation of 50% margins (don't just multiply by 1.5).
- Paragraph describing what similar objects sell for and then reasoning about whether you will be able to sell your light profitably.
