# Overview
It's time to show your final, high-fidelity prototype of your solution to others and get feedback. You will setup a table with your sketches prototypes, and final high-fidelity prototype and present your project to your peers and other members of the GIX community.

# Examples

# Learning Objectives
By the end of this project you will be able to:
- Brainstorm several ways to solve physical problems.
- Test your ideas using appropriate prototyping techniques.
- Articulate the value of your solution to potential customers.

# Constraints
- You must present your project during your assigned time so you can receive feedback from your peers.
- Your final product may not be a raw 3D print or raw lasercut. Further surface treatment, such as paint or finish, is required.

# Deliverables
- Portfolio quality PDF including the following:
    - Introductory paragraph describing the problem and your solution.
    - Images of your final project in use.
    - Process documentation
        - Images of all your early sketches (10+) exploring multiple solutions.
        - Images of your handmade prototypes for size and shape with notes about what you learned.
        - Images of your medium-fidelity prototypes to test functionality with notes about what you learned.
