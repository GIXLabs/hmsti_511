# Overview
Could you make and sell your final project profitably? Get a quote from a manufacturer of your choice for 50 of your final project idea (see [Manufacturing Quotes Assignment](manu_quotes.md)). Include the estimated shipping cost as part of the total cost. Also include an estimate of how long it would take to assemble and package, then multiply that by $25/hr. Then calculate what you would need to sell your item for to have 50% margins (Note: don't just multiply by 1.5, this will be wrong).

Write up your reasoning on whether you could sell your product at this price based on the pricing numbers you got during your final project presentation.

Will you actually try to sell your product?

# Learning Objectives
By the end of this activity you will be able to do the following:
- Evaluate the cost to produce one of your designs.
- Make realistic judgments about the feasibility of selling your product profitably.

# Constraints
- Your calculations must include all of the parts designed by you.
    - If you have a combination of lasercut and 3D printed parts, you may need to get quotes from separate vendors and add them together.
    - Don't include the cost of fasteners (like screws).

# Deliverables
In a single pdf:
- Screenshot of your quote for enough parts to make 50 of your design.
- Calculation of per unit cost without labor.
- Calculation of per unit cost with labor.
- Calculation of 50% margins (don't just multiply by 1.5).
- Paragraph describing what similar objects sell for, the prices suggested by reviewers during your final project presentation, and then reason about whether you will be able to sell your design profitably.
