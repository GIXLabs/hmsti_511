# Overview
Once you have finalized your prototype, validated its usefulness to customers, and determined what price point you will sell the product at, you will need to pass your design off to someone to produce it. In a larger company, this may be an engineering department who optimizes for the appropriate manufacturing approach, or if you're a startup it may be your contract manufacturer.

You will need to include CAD files, images of the prototype, desired color and surface finish, and desired materials. Much of this would need to be documented in an engineering document if sent to a contract manufacturer, but a text document specifying these details is sufficient for this class.

# Learning Objectives
By the end of this activty, you will be able to:
- Document your work so it can be picked up by someone else less familiar with your work.

# Constraints

# Deliverables
- Original CAD files
- A text document with the following:
    - Sufficient images of the final prototype to communicate use.
    - Description of color, material, and surface finish for each part.
