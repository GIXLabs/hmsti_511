# Overview
Products are usually made of more than one piece, which have to be joined in some way. This activity will give you some hands on experience with what it's like to use those joints for assembly.

# Learning Objectives
By the end of this experience you'll be able to:
- Describe several techniques for joining 2 pieces together.
- Choose an appropriate technique for the stage of prototyping you are at.

# Quiz Format
1. Assemble the clearance and pilot hole model with the provided self-tapping screws. This is the most common way you'll see pieces combined and so we'll treat it as the baseline to compare all the other methods to.
1. Assemble the unadjusted hole model with self-tapping screws.
    1. Does this one have a smaller or larger gap between the two parts than the baseline? Please explain why you think that is.
    1. Was this easier or harder to assemble than the baseline?
1. Assemble the heat set insert model with machine screws.
    1. Was this easier or harder to assemble than the baseline?
    1. How are the machine screws different than the self-tapping screws?
    1. Would the baseline model be improved by using machine screws? Why or why not?
1. Assemble the captive fastener model with machine screws.
    1. How easy was this to assemble relative to the heat set insert model?
    1. Does your answer change if you had to install the heat set inserts yourself?
1. Attempt to assemble the small boss with self-tapping screws and the big boss with self-tapping screws.
    1. Did the pieces go together successfully? If they did, explain what you think could have gone wrong. If they didn't, explain why.
1. Assemble and disassemble the horizontal snap pieces a few times.
    1. How tightly were the pieces held together after a few removals?
    1. Did anything break?
1. Assemble and disassemble the vertical snap pieces a few times.
    1. How tightly were the pieces held together after a few removals?
    1. Did anything break?
1. How would you rank these in terms of how easy they are to 3D model?
    - Easy to 3D model options are generally wise for early in the prototyping process so you don't waste much time modeling when there are many other unknowns to figure out.
1. How would you rank these in terms of reliable they are for repeated assembly and disassembly?
    - Reliability of assembly and disassembly may be something to prioritize when your uncertainty is around what goes inside your enclosure, such as the electrical hardware.
1. How would you rank these in terms of how easy they are to assemble?
    - Ease of assembly may be important when designing the final process because it can drive down the per unit assembly cost of your product.

# FAQs
- How are heat set inserts installed?
    - Using a heated tip, the brass insert is heated up and melted into the plastic. You must be careful to print with enough walls to have enough plastic to melt.
