# Overview
You should have several medium-fidelity models of the physical form your project will be taking for inspection and critical feedback. You will probably also have some higher-fidelity models/prototypes for inspection. For the crit, be prepared to show all of your prototype work in both digital and physical form for review (similar to the way we reviewed previous assignments). You should have made significant progress since your previous low-fidelity prototypes, reducing the uncertainty in your design.

- Several physical prototypes of various levels of fidelity
- Be organized and prepared to present your machine-fabricated physical models along with any sketches. Make sure you are able to justify design decisions and identify potential challenges.

# Learning Objectives
By the end of this activty, you will be able to:
- Apply the physical prototyping skills you have learned in this class to a real world problem.

# Constraints
- Each prototype must address a specific question you have about your product.
    - Ideally the answer should reduce a source of uncertainty in your design.
- You must bring all of your materials to class to participate in the critique.

# Deliverables
Single pdf (<10 MB) including:
- Any new sketches you've made   
- Images of at least 2 new medium-fidelity prototypes with notes
    - What is this image of?
    - Why did you make that prototype?
    - What did you learn?
- What is the biggest source of uncertainty now? Please be specific. "Whether it will work" is too vague.
